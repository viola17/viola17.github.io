  • Time Series Forecasting of Electricity Loading in Cape Town

    • Jiamin Zhong

    Multi-Dwelling Units (MDUs) are housing estates, such as apartment complexes, consisting of individual residences. Electricity use is monitored by a bulk meter which measures the total load for the entire MDU. The bulk meter records average power readings over 30 minute intervals which are used for billing and analysis of usage patterns. This project aims at training and analyzing predictions models, based on bulk meter readings and weather info between 10.14.2018 and 10.13.2019, that accurately forecast future electricity usage.

  • Comparison of Campus Crime with Crime in the Surrounding City

    • Yizhi Fang, Matt Welch, Jiamin Zhong, Jonathan Pickett

    Can a "dangerous" city actually provide safe study enviroment? Are "rich" cities safer? To answer these questions, we used FBI arrest dataset, median income by state dataset and campus crime dataset to analyze the relations between safety level of each campus to that of its surrounding cities. First, we looked into the crime data in the cities surrounding campuses and compared that with on campus weapon, drug, alcohol violations across the States in 2010. Next, We explored how crime rates, adjusted on a population level, relate to the median income levels of each state. Check code and datasets to see how we merged crime datasets by State and City and printed the least safe universities and summed the data on a state level to merge with the median income data by Sate.

  • Human Resources Analytics by Exploration Data Analysis and Modeling

    • Jiamin Zhong, Ruonan Zhang, Jiayi Geng

    Employees leave a company for variety of reasons, but some can be controlled. Is the low salary really the main reason that leads to high turnover rate? With careful data analysis and modeling, this project revealed that important factors that influence the turnover rate are usually satifaction level, workload and performance. We also provided useful suggestions to managers who want good employees to stay!

  • Automata and Space-Filling Curves

    • Ruoyu Meng, Ziqin Xiong, Hughes Lu, Jiamin Zhong

    In the last few years, novel connections between mathematical logic, automata theory and metric geomery have emerged. The goal of this project is to determine whether graphs of space-filling curves can be recognized by an automation, in a similar way where ternary representations in which the digit 1 does not occur of elements of a usual middle-thirds Cantor set C can be recognized by an automation.
